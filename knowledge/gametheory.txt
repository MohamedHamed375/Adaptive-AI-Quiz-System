Game theory studies strategic interactions between rational agents.
A game consists of players, actions, and payoffs.
Players choose strategies to maximize their own utility.
A Nash equilibrium occurs when no player benefits from changing strategy unilaterally.
Games can be cooperative or non-cooperative.
Zero-sum games involve one player's gain being another player's loss.
Payoff matrices represent outcomes for different strategy combinations.
Repeated games allow strategies to depend on past actions.
In repeated games, cooperation can emerge through future reward consideration.
Game theory is widely used in economics, AI, and multi-agent systems.
