Generative Adversarial Networks consist of a Generator and a Discriminator.
The generator produces synthetic data to fool the discriminator.
The discriminator evaluates whether data is real or generated.
GANs are trained using a minimax game between the generator and discriminator.
The generator improves by learning from discriminator feedback.
A common training issue in GANs is mode collapse.
GAN training is often unstable and sensitive to hyperparameters.
The discriminator should not become too strong early in training.
Wasserstein GAN improves training stability by modifying the loss function.
GANs are widely used in image generation and data augmentation.
