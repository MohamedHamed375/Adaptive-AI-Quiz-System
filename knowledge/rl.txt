Reinforcement Learning is a learning paradigm where an agent learns by interacting with an environment.
The agent receives rewards or penalties based on its actions.
The goal of reinforcement learning is to maximize cumulative reward over time.
An RL problem is typically modeled as a Markov Decision Process.
Exploration allows the agent to discover new actions, while exploitation uses known information.
The epsilon-greedy strategy balances exploration and exploitation.
Q-learning is a model-free reinforcement learning algorithm.
Q-learning updates action values based on observed rewards and future estimates.
The learning rate controls how quickly new information overrides old knowledge.
The discount factor determines the importance of future rewards.
